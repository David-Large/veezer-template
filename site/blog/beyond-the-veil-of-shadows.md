The veil first revealed itself at dusk, when the world’s colours thinned and the air grew heavy with unspoken memories. To most, it was nothing more than a trick of fading light, but to those who listened—truly listened—it whispered of another realm pressed tightly against their own. Beyond it lay a country of shadows not born of darkness, but of things forgotten: abandoned names, broken vows, and dreams set aside too soon. The veil rippled like a living thing, reacting to fear and longing in equal measure.

Arin crossed the threshold with a lantern forged of moon-silver, its flame steady where courage held firm. On the other side, the land bent in impossible ways—roads looping back on themselves, towers leaning as if to overhear secrets, and figures moving at the edge of sight. These were the Veiled Ones, keepers of the in-between, their faces obscured not by cloth but by shifting mist. They spoke in riddles, for plain truth had too much weight here and could fracture the realm if spoken aloud.

### The Mirror of Echoes

At the heart of the shadows stood the Mirror of Echoes, said to show not who you were, but who you might have been. As Arin approached, the lantern dimmed, fed now by resolve rather than flame. He understood then that the veil was not a barrier meant to keep mortals out, but a test meant to keep them honest. Those who fled their past were lost forever among the shadows; those who faced it could return, changed, carrying a fragment of the other world within them—proof that even in shadow, something enduring still waits to be found.
