The Windborn Isles drifted far above the waking seas, held aloft by ancient currents and the voices of the sky itself. Each island sang as it moved, low and resonant tones echoing through cloud and mist, carrying stories of voyages never finished and names lost to the horizon. Sailors below claimed the wind changed direction to follow these melodies, as if the world itself yearned to listen.

**The Sky Remembers**

Those who reached the isles learned that the songs were more than music—they were a living record, shaped by choice and motion:

* Every island held a different harmony, born from the hopes of those who first set foot upon it.
* The winds carried warnings as well as blessings, guiding the worthy and scattering the reckless.
* To add a verse to the song, one had to leave something behind—fear, ambition, or a name.

> When the isles finally shifted beyond sight, their music lingered, woven into the breath of the world. Long after the clouds closed and the sky fell silent, travelers still felt the pull of those unseen melodies, reminding them that some journeys never truly end—they simply learn how to fly.
